ABOUT ME

Guiding Principles

As a lifelong student, I am constantly trying to expand my understanding of people, places, concepts, skills, and anything in between. My motto, “stay curious,” is a humble reminder that there is always more to learn.

Sustainability

Truly sustainable solutions require a balance of the needs for the environment, people, and the economy. When too much emphasis is placed on one area, the other areas must compensate, leading to non-sustainable outcomes in the long run. It is through this balanced lens of sustainability that I approach problem-solving to create lasting solutions both professionally and personally.

Background

I graduated from the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) with a B.S. in Environmental Science and specialization in Environmental Engineering. As a scientist, I learned how to collect accurate and meaningful data to identify problems. Engineering pushes me to then design data-driven solutions for those problems. It was through my classes and student research positions that I was introduced to GIS and remote sensing. I fell in love with its possibility as a tool for data collection, analysis, and public education.

After graduation, I started working for Chesapeake Utilities as a GIS Specialist. I continue to expand my skills in remote sensing and GIS through ESRI Academy and guide personal learning on Youtube.
